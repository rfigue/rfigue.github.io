Greetings and welcome to our RocketLynx team web page!

Here you will find content and documentation on current and future projects that we'll be working on.

As of right now, we intent to participate in the Spaceport America Cup 2020 where we'll be competing against top colleges in the nation. 
We are a small group of engineers and computer scientist based out of University of Colorado Denver.

The long-term objective of RocketLynx is to enter, compete, and successfully obtain an award in the Intercollegiate Rocket Engineering Competition, IREC. This competition is hosted by the Experimental Sounding Rocket Association, ESRA, as part of a week-long event known as the Spaceport America Cup that takes place in southern New Mexico.

Our short-term objective of RocketLynx is to design a propulsion system and an airframe for the capstone senior design project as part of the mechanical engineering curriculum at the University of Colorado-Denver.

The RocketLynx project is an initiative taken mostly by upper-level mechanical engineering students of the University of Colorado-Denver. The team consists of students and affiliates of different backgrounds: computer science; electrical, aerospace, and civil engineering; hobbyist rocketeers; social sciences; and visual arts.
